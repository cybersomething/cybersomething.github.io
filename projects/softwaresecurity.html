Engineering Resilient Systems - Software Security

This is the report for the software security submodule that I produced for the Engineering Resilient Systems module. If you would like to see the report, click here. Please don't plaigirise as you'll likely get caught and it's not a good look :).


Feedback ============ Overall: ============ OK - What you've done is well written and well presented but you've fallen into the trap of thinking of this like an ethical hacker and not as a software engineer. What you've written is all perfectly correct, but it doesn't say much about the *development process* of the software, rather you've focused on the task of deployment and system management. Use of CWE/CVE ============ Some good research on the CWE/CVE DB with relevant vulnerabilities found. Consideration of a *class* of vulnerabilities ============================================ A good consideration of the *class* of vulnerabilities as a whole. Integration with the existing SE processes ============================================ You could maybe have said a little bit more about how your recommendation could be integrated into the company's existing Software Engineering process (use of CI approach - source code control system - process - training etc.) I'd like to have seen a bit more about who does what, when. Demonstrating the technique will solve the problem ============== A well-reasoned discussion but one that is slightly off-topic for the assignment. I think it's interesting that you called section 5 "Mitigation" - which implies it's about fixing the problems after they've been created. The assignment was really about how to avoid creating the problems in the first place.
